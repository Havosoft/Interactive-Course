Safe Use of Wi-Fi & Hotspots

Protect company devices and data while working remotely or in public places

Learning objectives:
  • Understand the risks associated with public and unsecured Wi-Fi networks.
  • Recognize potential attacks such as eavesdropping, man-in-the-middle, and rogue hotspots.
  • Apply secure practices when connecting to Wi-Fi outside the office.
  • Protect company devices and data while working remotely or in public spaces.

Introduction

With the rise of remote work and mobile computing, employees often rely on Wi-Fi networks in airports, hotels, coffee shops, and other public locations. While convenient, public Wi-Fi can expose sensitive information if not used securely. Attackers can intercept data, impersonate networks, or inject malware.

Why Safe Wi-Fi Practices Matter

  • Data Interception: Unencrypted Wi-Fi allows attackers to capture emails, passwords, or business documents.
  • Rogue Hotspots: Cybercriminals can set up fake networks with names similar to legitimate ones to trick users.
  • Man-in-the-Middle (MITM) Attacks: Attackers can intercept communication between your device and the network, altering or stealing data.
  • Device Exploitation: Public networks can be used to scan and compromise connected devices.

Employees must understand the risks and adopt safe practices to protect corporate data outside the office.

1. Use a VPN

Always connect to a secure VPN when accessing corporate networks from public Wi-Fi. VPNs encrypt data, making it unreadable to attackers.

2. Verify Network Names

Confirm the official Wi-Fi name with staff at hotels, airports, or caf√©s. Avoid networks with generic or suspicious names.

3. Enable HTTPS

Ensure websites and applications use HTTPS to encrypt communication. Avoid transmitting sensitive data over unencrypted channels.

4. Disable Automatic Connections

Turn off settings that automatically connect your device to available Wi-Fi networks.

5. Device Security

Keep operating systems, browsers, and security software updated. Enable firewalls and disable file sharing when on public networks.

6. Avoid Sensitive Transactions

Refrain from accessing financial systems, confidential emails, or critical company applications on public Wi-Fi unless connected via VPN.

Conclusion & Takeaway

Public Wi-Fi is convenient but inherently risky. Cyber attackers can intercept communications, trick users into connecting to fake networks, or exploit vulnerabilities on connected devices. By using VPNs, verifying network authenticity, enabling HTTPS, and following device security best practices, employees can safely access corporate resources outside the office. Vigilance is essential to maintain data security in public spaces.
