Introduction

Attackers harvest publicly available professional and technical information from platforms such as LinkedIn, GitHub, and X. That intelligence enables highly personalised spear-phishing and impersonation attacks. This module helps you recognize and reduce exposure.

  • Harvesting roles and teams to craft targeted emails
  • Scanning repos for secrets or tool names
  • Impersonation of recruiters/executives to build trust

We'll walk through five realistic use cases and give actionable guidance to reduce risk.

Use Cases — choose an action and see outcomes

Best Practices

  • Profile hygiene: limit public details about role responsibility and systems.
  • GitHub: never commit secrets; enable secret scanning and .gitignore.
  • Verify recruiters and contacts via secondary channels before sharing attachments.
  • Adjust privacy settings and avoid posting internal project specifics.
  • Report impersonation and suspicious DMs to IT/security and the platform.

Takeaway

Attackers use social platforms to gather high-fidelity intelligence that enables spear-phishing. Reduce exposure by limiting oversharing, locking down code and repos, verifying contacts, and training staff to spot personalised phishing attempts.
