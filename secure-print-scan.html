Introduction

Multifunction printers (MFPs) — print, scan, fax devices — are networked endpoints that often store copies of documents, connect to corporate email and file systems, and can become points of data leakage if not secured.

  • They store digital copies of printed/scanned jobs.
  • They connect to networks and can be remotely accessed if misconfigured.
  • Their hard drives may contain thousands of sensitive documents.

This module covers common risks (uncollected printouts, unsecured scan-to-email, exposed HDDs), use-case decisions, and mitigation steps.

Best Practices for Secure Print & Scan

  • Secure Print Release: use PIN or badge to release jobs.
  • Encrypt scan-to-mail and restrict recipients.
  • Harden MFP admin interfaces and disable unused protocols.
  • Wipe or destroy internal storage before disposal.
  • Log & monitor printing and scanning activity.
  • Disable or restrict scan-to-USB and other risky functions.

Takeaway

Printers and scanners are networked devices that handle sensitive data. Use secure print release, encrypted scan workflows, access controls, device hardening, monitoring, and secure disposal to reduce risk.
