Topic 1: Workplace Access Control

Physical entry security and best practices

Learning Objectives:
  • Understand the importance of controlling physical access to the workplace.
  • Apply best practices for securing entry points and sensitive areas.
  • Recognize the risks associated with unauthorized access.
  • Follow policies for granting, monitoring, and revoking access credentials.

Introduction

Workplace access control is a fundamental aspect of physical security. Protecting entry points, sensitive areas, and restricted facilities prevents unauthorized personnel from gaining access to company assets, confidential information, or critical systems. Even with robust cybersecurity measures, physical breaches can compromise overall security.

Why Access Control Matters

  • Prevent Unauthorized Entry: Restricts access to sensitive areas to authorized personnel only.
  • Protect Assets: Safeguards physical devices, documents, and intellectual property.
  • Reduce Insider Threats: Monitors and logs employee movements to detect suspicious behavior.
  • Support Incident Response: Enables tracking and investigation of physical security breaches.

1. Controlled Entry Points
Secure all doors, gates, turnstiles, and entryways. Use electronic access control systems with logging capabilities.

2. Multi-Factor Physical Authentication
Combine badges, PINs, and biometrics for sensitive areas. Regularly review and update authentication methods.

3. Visitor Management
Pre-register visitors and issue temporary badges. Escort visitors in restricted areas and maintain a log.

4. Access Monitoring & Auditing
Track who enters and exits critical areas. Conduct periodic audits to ensure access permissions are up-to-date.

5. Policy for Lost or Stolen Credentials
Immediately report lost badges or access devices. Deactivate compromised credentials and issue replacements.

6. Employee Awareness
Educate staff on the importance of not letting unauthorized personnel enter secure areas. Encourage reporting of suspicious activity or policy violations.

Conclusion

Workplace access control is a cornerstone of physical security. Properly managed entry systems, monitored access, and employee vigilance prevent unauthorized access, protect company assets, and support incident response. Every employee has a role in maintaining secure environments.
