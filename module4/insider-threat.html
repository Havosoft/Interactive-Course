Topic 3: Insider Threat Awareness

Recognizing, reporting, and preventing insider risks

Learning Objectives:
  • Understand the different types of insider threats (malicious, negligent, and compromised).
  • Recognize behaviors and signs that may indicate insider risks.
  • Apply best practices to minimize insider threats.
  • Report suspicious activity to security or HR teams.
  • Support a culture of vigilance and accountability in the workplace.
Progress

Introduction

Insider threats involve employees, contractors, or trusted partners who misuse access to company resources, either intentionally or accidentally. These threats can result in data breaches, financial loss, reputational damage, or operational disruption. Unlike external attacks, insider threats exploit legitimate access, making them harder to detect.

Why Insider Threat Awareness Matters

  • High Risk: Insiders already have access to sensitive systems, making attacks more effective.
  • Costly Consequences: Insider incidents are among the most expensive security breaches.
  • Early Detection: Awareness helps identify potential risks before damage occurs.
  • Support for Security Culture: Encourages employees to take shared responsibility for protecting assets.

1. Access Management
Assign permissions based on roles (least privilege principle). Revoke access immediately when employees leave or change roles.

2. Continuous Monitoring
Track system access and file activity for anomalies. Use logs and alerts to identify unusual behavior.

3. Employee Training & Awareness
Educate staff on phishing, social engineering, and proper data handling. Promote vigilance and encourage reporting of suspicious activities.

4. Data Handling Policies
Clearly define acceptable use of company devices and data. Implement encryption and DLP tools for sensitive information.

5. Reporting Mechanisms
Provide clear channels for reporting suspicious or risky behavior. Encourage anonymity if necessary to ensure comfort in reporting.

6. Physical Security Integration
Monitor insider activity with access control logs and surveillance. Combine physical and digital monitoring to detect irregular behavior.
