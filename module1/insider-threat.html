Introduction

An insider threat comes from someone within the organization â€” an employee, contractor or partner â€” who compromises security either deliberately or accidentally. Because insiders often have legitimate access, their actions can bypass many controls and cause serious harm.

Why it matters

  • High Impact: Insiders often have legitimate access, making it easier to bypass security controls.
  • Difficult to Detect: Insider activity can blend in with normal business operations.
  • Financial and Reputational Damage: Insider breaches can lead to legal penalties, regulatory fines, and loss of customer trust.
  • Combination with External Threats: Insiders may collaborate with external attackers, amplifying risks.

  • Least Privilege: Provide access to sensitive systems only to employees who need it to perform their job. Regularly review and update access permissions.
  • Monitoring & Logging: Implement systems to monitor unusual activity on network, email, and data storage systems. Review logs periodically to detect potential threats.
  • Training & Awareness: Train staff to recognize insider threat indicators, including unusual data access or policy violations. Encourage employees to report suspicious behavior without fear of retaliation.
  • Secure Credentials & Devices: Enforce password hygiene, MFA, and proper workstation security. Ensure company devices are not used for personal storage or unapproved software.
  • Incident Reporting & Response: Establish clear procedures for reporting suspected insider activity. Investigate incidents promptly while preserving evidence.
  • Background Checks:Conduct thorough pre-employment screening for sensitive roles. Regularly reassess risk for contractors and employees in critical positions.
